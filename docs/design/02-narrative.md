# DIVINE DOMINION — Narrative & Meta-Progression

> Cross-references: [Overview](01-overview.md) · [Commandments](03-commandments.md) · [Eras](07-eras-and-endgame.md) · [Harbinger](14-harbinger.md) · [Era Narratives](02b-era-narratives.md) · [INDEX](../INDEX.md)

---

## Narrative Tone

**Mythic with dark humor** (Pratchett, Hades). Dramatic but self-aware, wry but not glib. The narrator is the player's god — an ancient being who's seen too many worlds burn and developed opinions about it. Big moments earn genuine emotional weight; quiet moments earn a smirk. Never pompous, never nihilistic.

---

## The Multiverse Frame

Your God has no known origin. You arrive on an Earth. You establish a religion. You have done this before — perhaps countless times. You do not remember your first Earth. You only know the cycle: arrive, choose, guide, survive or fail.

---

## Origin Story

There is no canonical backstory. The player's God is unnamed and unexplained. The mystery is intentional: Are you a refugee? A creator? A wanderer? The game never answers. What matters is the present Earth and the choices you make.

---

## When You Lose: New Earth

**When aliens destroy Earth:** You wake up on a new Earth. Time resets to 1600. The people are different. The nations are different. The rival religions are different. But you remember.

**When civilization destroys itself (nuclear war):** Same outcome. "They always do this." New Earth. Fresh start.

| Persists | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **God's memory** | Lifetime stats: Earths saved, Earths lost, total playtime, interventions cast |
| **Unlocked commandments** | All 35 base available from start. 15 unlock through milestones (surviving past 1900, winning, losing 3 times, winning pure peace, winning pure war, visiting 10 Earths). 50 total |
| **Knowledge of alien threat** | Narrative callbacks in later runs. "You've seen this before." |
| **Titles and achievements** | Cosmetic and bragging rights. "Lone Guardian," "Peacemaker," "Survivor" |

## What Resets Each Run

| Resets | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| World map | Geography, nations, borders |
| Rival religions | Different faiths, different commandments |
| Your religion | You choose 10 commandments again |
| Technology | Back to 1600s level |
| Influence / territory | Your faith starts from zero |

---

## Roguelite Loop

Each 4-hour run is fresh. Meta-knowledge grows. A player on their 5th Earth may choose commandments to fast-track science — because they *know* what's coming. The game rewards learning the hidden win condition without spoiling it for first-time players.

---

## Win / Lose Conditions (6 Outcomes)

| Outcome | Type | Description |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **United Front** | Victory | Defense Grid online + 3+ nations cooperating. Humanity stands together. |
| **Lone Guardian** | Victory | Defense Grid via single superpower. One nation carries the world. |
| **Survival** | Partial Victory | Defense Grid partially online. Massive devastation. Humanity survives, barely. |
| **Extinction** | Loss | No defense. Earth falls. "Again." |
| **Self-Destruction** | Loss | Nuclear war destroys civilization before aliens arrive. "They always do this." |
| **Ascension** | Secret | Max science + max peace + max faith. Humanity transcends. Meets aliens as equals. |

## First-Run Intro Sequence

> Exact text shown when starting the first Earth. Establishes identity, stakes, and tone.

*You have been here before.*

*You don't remember when, or how many times. You only know the feeling: a world taking shape beneath you, fragile and temporary and full of extremely confident primates.*

*This is Earth. One of many. Yours to guide — or to ruin. The line between those two things is thinner than you'd expect.*

*Choose your commandments. They will be your voice, your doctrine, the shape of faith you carve into this world. Choose wisely, or don't. Wisdom is overrated when you have divine lightning.*

*Build a religion. Spread it. Defend it. Watch it fracture and reform across six hundred years of war, plague, invention, and deeply questionable political decisions.*

*There are other gods here. Sleeping ones, passive ones. You are the only one who bothers showing up.*

*Something is coming. You won't know what for a long time. When you do, you'll wish you'd prepared.*

*Begin.*

> Gradual reveal from ~1950 onward. First-timers experience growing unease; veterans recognize the signs.

| 1 | 1950 | Strange Patterns | *Astronomers report irregularities in the stellar background. Stars that shouldn't flicker do. Stars that should don't. Probably nothing. Astronomers report a lot of things.* |

## Ending Narratives (6 Types + 1 Special)

### United Front (Victory)
*They came for your world and found it waiting. Not one nation, but many — armed, prepared, defiant. Your commandments united enough of them, your interventions nudged enough of the rest. The Defense Grid held. Humanity breathes. You allow yourself a moment of something resembling pride. Just a moment. There are other Earths that might not be so fortunate.*

### Lone Guardian (Victory)
*One nation carried the world. One magnificent, terrifying superpower, forged by your doctrine and fueled by your blessings, built a wall that held back the void. The rest of humanity owes everything to a civilization they probably feared more than the aliens. Irony is eternal.*

### Survival (Partial Victory)
*The grid held — mostly. The defense was ragged, improvised, desperate. Billions died. But some lived. They'll rebuild, scarred and changed and wondering why their god let it get this bad. You wonder the same thing.*

### Extinction (Loss)
*They weren't ready. You know this feeling — the silence after the noise, the void where a world used to be. It's not your first loss. It won't be your last. Again.*

### Self-Destruction (Loss)
*They didn't even make it to the invasion. Nuclear fire, launched by one of your most devout nations, ended the argument about alien defense by ending everything else. You watch the ashes cool. They always do this.*

### Ascension (Secret Victory)
*You didn't plan this. Neither did they. But somewhere between the commandments and the centuries, between the wars you stopped and the plagues you didn't, humanity became something else. When the aliens arrived, they didn't find prey. They found equals. The conversation that followed was... interesting.*

### Abandoned Followers (Special)

**Trigger:** Tracked via `abandonmentScore`. Increments: +1 per auto-resolved event, +3 per era with 0 blessings/disasters/whispers. Resets to 0 on any divine action (blessing, disaster, or whisper). Threshold: `ABANDONMENT_THRESHOLD = 15`. A player who ignores 15 events in a row, or goes 5 idle eras, triggers this ending. See `constants.md`.

*You stopped caring, didn't you? Somewhere around era {abandonment_era}, when the schisms piled up and the wars got repetitive, you just... stopped answering prayers. Your followers noticed. They built the Defense Grid anyway — or they didn't. Either way, they did it without you. That might be the cruelest miracle of all.*

## Narrative Tone Across Runs

| Run | Tone |
|-----|------|
| **First Earth** | Discovery. You learn the world, the mechanics, the stakes. No hints about aliens until ~1950. Dark humor emerges in events and quiet moments. |
| **Later Earths** | Callbacks. "You've seen this before." God acknowledges the cycle. Narrative acknowledges your experience. Humor becomes more resigned, more knowing. |

The game does not punish failure with shame. It frames each loss as another chance. "Again." is resigned, not cruel. The loop is the point.
