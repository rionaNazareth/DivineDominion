## 1. Vision Statement

DIVINE DOMINION is a mobile god game where you create a religion by choosing 10 commandments, then watch — and subtly guide — 600 years of civilization unfold on a living vector map, all compressed into a single 4-hour run. You are the only active God in a world of passive deities. Bless your followers, unleash disasters on your enemies, and push humanity toward space-age technology before an alien invasion no one sees coming. Each 4-hour run is a fresh Earth with new nations, new rivals, and new ways to fail. When you lose, you awaken on another Earth, carrying only memory and unlocked commandments. It's Civilization meets Black & White meets a roguelite — designed for 15-minute phone sessions.

**One-liner:** *Create a religion. Guide civilization. Save the world — or watch it burn.*

## 2. Design Pillars

| # | Pillar | Description | This means... |
|---|--------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | **You Write the Rules** | 10 commandments mechanically shape how your religion thinks, fights, researches, and treats outsiders. | Picking "Knowledge Above All" makes your followers build universities instead of temples. Picking "Holy War" makes them crusade. Commandments are not flavor text — they are the game's core variable. |
| 2 | **Active God in a Passive Pantheon** | You are the only deity who intervenes. Rival religions have hidden divine rules that produce "miracles," but their Gods never act. An alien intelligence — the Harbinger — works against you from the shadows, but it is not a god. It has no worshippers, no faith. It can only disrupt. | You can flood a hostile nation mid-war. Their god can't respond. But their hidden rules might make their followers resilient to floods — you won't know until you try. And starting in Era 7, something else is working against you — something that isn't divine at all. |
| 3 | **Humans Being Humans** | Followers interpret, argue, reform, and betray your commandments. The real challenge isn't rival Gods — it's people. | You decree "Thou Shalt Not Kill." Your followers start a war anyway, rationalize it as "defense," and your religion schisms over the contradiction. |
| 4 | **The Clock You Don't See** | An alien fleet is approaching Earth — and they've already sent a scout. The Harbinger, a transmitted alien intelligence, sabotages humanity from within starting Era 7. First-time players don't know about either threat. | New players focus on religion dominance. Veterans know about the Harbinger and prepare from Era 1 — building prosperity for resistance, positioning Follower Voices as scouts. This meta-knowledge transforms repeat plays entirely. |
| 5 | **Infinite Earths, Finite Sessions** | Each 4-hour run is a complete story on a fresh world. Lose = new Earth. 15-minute sessions fit a commute. | Pick up your phone, handle 2 events and a blessing, put it down. Or sink an hour into managing a world war. Both are valid sessions. |

## 3. What Makes This Different

| vs. | DIVINE DOMINION | The Other Game |
|-----|-----------------|----------------|
| **Civilization** | You're a God, not a nation. You don't build cities — you shape beliefs. Win = humanity survives aliens, not domination. | 4X empire-building, direct control of units and cities. |
| **God Games (Black & White)** | No creature training. No direct miracles on individuals. Focus is macro: religion design, civilization-scale events, and the hidden alien threat. | Creature AI, direct miracles, village-scale interaction. |
| **Plague Inc** | You're saving humanity, not destroying it. Religion and civilization dynamics, not pathogen spread. Win = unite and defend. | Pathogen simulation, global infection, humanity as target. |

## 4. Core Gameplay Loop

1. **Watch** — The world map evolves in real-time. Nations rise and fall. Religions spread. Technology advances.
2. **Whisper** — Nudge nations with free micro-interventions (war, peace, science, faith). Fill passive gaps with constant subtle influence.
3. **Respond** — Wars, schisms, discoveries, rebellions, and follower petitions demand your attention.
4. **Cast** — Divine blessings or disasters on regions via the dual-arc FAB. Powers unlock era-by-era. Trigger dramatic combos when powers interact with world state.
5. **Guide** — Push scientific progress. Your commandments shape research speed, cooperation, and priorities. Named followers champion your cause — or betray it.
6. **Defend** (Eras 7+) — The Harbinger begins sabotaging humanity. Counter its interference while building toward the Defense Grid.
7. **Survive** — The alien invasion arrives. Humanity must be ready — or you wake on another Earth.

## 5. Target Player Persona

**Name:** Alex, 30 — Strategy Gamer on the Go

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Age** | 25–40 |
| **Platform** | iPhone or Android. Plays on commute, lunch break, before bed. |
| **Gaming background** | Played Civilization (loves it, can't commit 8 hours). Enjoyed Plague Inc, Mini Metro, Reigns, Polytopia. Tried idle games but wants more agency. Has a Switch but rarely uses it. |
| **Session pattern** | 1–2 sessions daily, 10–20 min each. Longer on weekends (30–60 min). 12–16 sessions per Earth (1–2 weeks). |
| **Spending** | Willing to pay $3–7 after trying a quality mobile experience. Buys Polytopia DLC, paid for Mini Metro. Won't pay for energy refills or loot boxes. Expects to try before buying. |
| **Motivation** | Explorer / Achiever (Bartle). Loves emergent stories ("you won't believe what happened in my run"). Wants to feel clever, not just skilled. Shares moments, not scores. |
| **What "fun" means** | Watching a plan unfold — or spectacularly backfire. Making a choice that echoes for centuries. Telling a friend about a war their religion accidentally started. |

**Not our player:** Competitive PvP gamers. Twitch-reflex players. People who need leaderboards to stay motivated.

## 6. Session Design

### First Session (Minute by Minute)

| Min | What Happens |
|-----|-------------|
| 0:00 | **Black screen.** "You have arrived." Ambient hum. |
| 0:30 | **Earth fades in** — smooth vector globe. "This is your Earth. It needs a God." |
| 1:00 | **Archetype selection.** Three cards: "The Shepherd" (peace/growth), "The Judge" (order/justice), "The Conqueror" (expansion/war). Each shows a philosophy and 3 sample commandments. Player picks one → 10 commandments pre-filled. |
| 2:00 | **Customize.** "These are your laws. Change any that don't feel right." Tap a commandment → see alternatives in that category. Game is paused; no timer pressure. |
| 3:30 | **World revealed.** Map zooms in. First followers appear in a small region, glowing gold. Nations and rival religions visible. |
| 4:00 | **Tutorial: "Tap your followers."** Region info panel slides up — population, faith, development. |
| 4:30 | **"Bless them."** FAB pulses. Player taps → dual-arc opens → Bountiful Harvest (only 2 powers available in Era 1). Golden wheat waves ripple. Population growth ticks up. |
| 5:00 | **AHA MOMENT.** Player sees their blessing's visible effect on the map. They feel like a God for the first time. |
| 5:30 | **World unpauses.** Religions spread. Nations trade. Era 1 begins. |
| 7:00 | **First event.** Toast notification: "A neighboring nation eyes your followers." Tap → event card with 2 choices. |
| 10:00 | **Religion spreading.** Gold influence creeps into adjacent regions without intervention. |
| 10:00+ | **First voice emerges** (if Prophet cast or war triggers it). "A voice rises from your followers." Named character appears on map. |
| 15:00 | **First conflict.** A nation attacks your followers. Battle plays on map. Player can intervene (storm on attackers), whisper peace, or watch. |
| 20:00 | **Natural stopping point.** Era 1 ends. Summary card: "The Renaissance ends. Your faith has X followers across Y regions." New power unlocks. |

### Returning Session

The game pauses when closed — no offline progression. On reopen:

1. Current state summary: followers, active wars, pending events, divine energy stored.
2. Player taps "Continue" → world unpauses, queued events fire.

### Session 2 Hook

"Your religion spread into 3 new regions, but a rival faith is pushing back. A prophet appeared in the east. You have 8 Divine Energy — what will you do with it?"

The hook is **unresolved tension** + **accumulated resources**. The player left mid-story and has power to spend.

### Pause / Resume

| Saved | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Full world state | Nations, religions, armies, trade, disease, science |
| Event queue | Pending events awaiting response |
| Divine energy | Current amount + regen progress |
| Camera & speed | Position, zoom level, speed setting |
| Era progress | Exact game-year within current era |

No time passes while paused. Resume shows the map exactly as left.

## 7. First-Time User Experience (FTUE)

### Screen Sequence

1. **Void** — Black screen, ambient tone. "You have arrived." (2s)
2. **Earth** — Planet fades in, rotating. "This is your Earth. It needs a God." (3s)
3. **Archetype Selection** — Three illustrated cards: Shepherd, Judge, Conqueror. One-line philosophy + 3 sample commandments each. Player picks one. (Player-paced, no timer.) All 3 archetypes are free.
4. **Commandment Review** — 10 pre-selected commandments with swap option. Each shows mechanical effect in plain language. (Player-paced.)
5. **World Map** — Zoom to continent. Followers appear glowing gold. Tutorial overlay points to your region.
6. **First Blessing** — FAB pulses. Guided: tap FAB → dual-arc opens (2 powers: Bountiful Harvest + Great Storm) → tap Harvest → tap region. VFX plays. "Your people prosper."
7. **Release** — Tutorial fades. World unpauses. "Now watch. Intervene when you must."

### Explained vs. Discovered

| Taught (Tutorial) | Discovered (Play) |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| How to cast a blessing (via FAB dual-arc) | What disasters do |
| How to read the region panel | How commandments shape nation behavior |
| That Divine Energy exists | That it has a cap and regen rate |
| That events need responses | That ignoring events has consequences |
| How to pause (visible button + two-finger tap) | The Divine Overlay (Eye at FAB apex) |
| — | Divine Whispers (bottom sheet, after tutorial) |
| — | Power Combos (discovered through play) |
| — | Follower Voices (emerge from simulation ~minute 10) |
| — | The alien threat (~Era 9) |
| — | Schisms from contradictory actions |
| — | That commandment choice affects win path |

### The "Aha Moment"

**When:** ~Minute 5 of the first session.
**What:** Player casts Bountiful Harvest. Golden wheat waves ripple across the map. Population ticks up. The region glows brighter.
**Why it works:** The first moment the player feels like a god — not reading mechanics, but *seeing* divine will manifest on the living map. Visual, immediate, consequential.

### Commandment Onboarding

- **First Earth:** 3 archetype starter packs (Shepherd / Judge / Conqueror), each with 10 pre-selected commandments. Player can swap individual commandments before confirming. Reduces "pick 10 from 50" to "review 10, maybe swap 2–3."
- **Subsequent Earths:** Full commandment browser. Categories on the left, commandments on the right. Previously used ones highlighted. Newly unlocked ones marked with a glow.
- **Progressive unlock:** First Earth has all 35 base commandments. 15 more unlock through play milestones (survive past 1900, win via peace, win via war, etc.). 50 total.

## 8. Retention Pillars

### Why Players Start a New Earth

| # | Reason | How It Works |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **"What if I'd picked different commandments?"** | A war-focused run feels nothing like a peace-focused one. The system supports 10+ meaningfully different builds. |
| 2 | **"Now I know the real threat"** | After the first alien loss, players replay with meta-knowledge — prioritize science, push cooperation, experience a different strategic layer. |
| 3 | **"I was so close"** | Near-miss psychology. "If I'd blessed that science city one more time..." The game creates close outcomes to drive "one more run." |
| 4 | **"I unlocked new commandments"** | New commandments unlock after milestones. Each changes available strategies. |
| 5 | **"I want the secret ending"** | Ascension (max science + max peace + max faith) is hard and rare — a long-tail goal for dedicated players. |

### Meta-Progression Across Earths

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Commandment unlocks** | All 35 base available from start. 15 unlockable through milestones (survive past 1900, win, lose 3×, win pure peace, win pure war, visit 10 Earths). 50 total. |
| **God Profile** | Lifetime stats: Earths saved/lost, total interventions, followers converted, disasters unleashed, play time. |
| **Titles** | Earned labels displayed on profile and shareable cards: "Peacemaker," "Destroyer," "Lone Guardian," "Ascended." |
| **Earth History** | In-game history of each run — commandments chosen, pivotal moments (wars, schisms, miracles), achievements unlocked. Viewable during and after each Earth. Players see their story unfold. *(MVP-essential)* |
| **Narrative callbacks** | Later runs include text: "You've seen this before." "The stars look the same on every Earth." |

### Daily / Weekly Hooks

**None.** Premium game, no live-service mechanics. No daily login rewards, no FOMO timers, no battle passes. Players return because the game is worth returning to.

### Earth #5 vs. Earth #1

| Earth #1 | Earth #5 |
|----------|----------|
| 35 base commandments | 40–45 unlocked (including unlockables) |
| No knowledge of aliens | Knows the timeline, prepares from Era 1 |
| Follows tutorial | Skips tutorial, goes straight to full builder |
| Picks an archetype | Crafts a custom set for a specific strategy |
| Reacts to events | Anticipates patterns, positions preemptively |
| "What is this?" | "Can I get Ascension this time?" |

## 10. Core Loop Timing Validation

### Is 4 Hours Per Earth Correct?

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total real-time | 4 hours (240 min) |
| Eras | 12 × 20 min each |
| Game years per minute | ~2.5 |
| Events per era (est.) | 8–15 (one every 1–2 min) |
| Sessions per Earth | 12–16 (at 15–20 min/session) |
| Calendar days per Earth | 6–16 days (1–2 sessions/day) |

**Verdict:** Correct. Long enough for an epic 600-year arc, short enough that losing doesn't devastate (vs. losing a 40-hour Civ game). At 15–20 min sessions, one Earth takes 1–2 weeks — ideal mobile roguelite cadence.

### Progressive Session Design *(human-confirmed)*

Sessions start short and dense to hook players, then gradually extend as investment grows:

| Era Range | Session Length | Event Density | Feel |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|------|
| Eras 1-3 (1600-1750) | ~10-12 min | 12-15 events/era | Rapid fire — constant payoff, immediate god-power feedback |
| Eras 4-6 (1750-1920) | ~15-18 min | 8-12 events/era | Strategic — wars develop, alliances shift, player settles in |
| Eras 7-9 (1920-2050) | ~18-20 min | 8-10 events/era | Tension — alien hints, bigger stakes per choice |
| Eras 10-12 (2050-2200) | ~20-25 min | 6-8 events/era | High stakes — fewer but heavier decisions, player is invested |

Natural stopping points at every era boundary. Early eras grab attention on a bus ride; late eras reward a committed player.

### Are 12 Eras Correct?

Each era has distinct flavor (Renaissance → Exploration → ... → Arrival). A new theme every 20 minutes prevents the "long middle" where nothing changes. Fewer (8) would make each era too long and sparse. More (16) would make transitions trivial.

**Verdict:** 12 eras is correct.

### MVP vs. Full Duration

MVP ships at **2 hours** (compressed pacing, same 12 eras at ~10 min each). Full vision is **4 hours** (12 eras × 20 min). The session math above uses full-vision timing. At MVP pacing, sessions per Earth drop to 6–8 — still a satisfying 1-week cadence. The 2→4 hour expansion is a tuning knob, not a structural change.

## Key Concepts

- **Commandments** — The 10 rules you choose at game start. Each has mechanical effects and behavior tags. See [Commandments](03-commandments.md).
- **Roguelite Loop** — Each Earth is a fresh run. Meta-progression unlocks more commandments and carries memory. See [Narrative](02-narrative.md).
- **Divine Whispers** — Free micro-nudges (war, peace, science, faith) cast on regions via the bottom sheet. Fill passive gaps. See [Divine Powers](06-divine-powers.md).
- **Power Combos** — Chain reactions when divine powers interact with world state. Discovered, not taught. See [Divine Powers](06-divine-powers.md).
- **Follower Voices** — Named characters who emerge from the simulation, petition for divine aid, and add emotional stakes. See [Follower Voices](13-follower-voices.md).
- **The Harbinger** — A transmitted alien intelligence that sabotages humanity from Era 7 onward. Not a god — no worshippers, no blessings. It amplifies existing tensions, adapts to your strategy, and gets stronger as the fleet approaches. See [Harbinger](14-harbinger.md).
- **Alien Invasion** — Hidden win/lose condition. The fleet is coming, and it sent a scout ahead. Humanity must reach space-age tech and build a Defense Grid to survive. See [Eras & Endgame](07-eras-and-endgame.md).
