## Time Scale

The game compresses 600 years (1600–2200) into ~4 hours of real-time play.

| Real Time | Game Time | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| 1 sec | ~2.5 days | Tick granularity |
| 1 min | ~2.5 years | Standard pacing |
| 10 min | ~25 years | Era transition scale |
| 1 hour | ~150 years | Major arc |
| 4 hours | ~600 years | Full game |

## Hidden Alien Clock

The player is **never told directly** about the alien threat at game start. Discovery is emergent through events and atmosphere.

### Notification Timeline (Approximate)

| Game Year | Player Experience |
|-----------|-------------------|
| ~1950 | "Strange patterns in the stars" — minor event, easily dismissed |
| ~2000 | "Message from beyond" — cryptic signal, scientists puzzled |
| ~2050 | "Something is coming" — consensus grows, governments react |
| ~2100 | "They will arrive within a lifetime" — urgency peaks |
| ~2150 | Full reveal — panic, mobilization, last preparations |
| ~2200 | Arrival — final outcome |

The clock advances based on game time, not player actions. Player actions determine whether humanity is *ready* when they arrive.

### The Harbinger (Advance Scout)

The alien fleet didn't just send a signal — they sent an intelligence ahead. The Harbinger is a transmitted alien AI that arrives at Era 7 and actively sabotages humanity's progress. It is NOT a god: no worshippers, no blessings, only disruption. Its Signal Strength (action budget) grows as the fleet approaches: 3 at Era 7, up to 25 at Era 12. It adapts to the player's strategy, targeting their strongest path to the Defense Grid. Follower Voices sense it at Era 8, the player confirms its existence at Era 9, and a new Anomaly overlay layer unlocks at Era 10. Full details in [Harbinger](14-harbinger.md).

## Science Progress (Global Emergent)

Science milestones are **global** — they emerge from nation development, not direct player control. Player influences them indirectly via commandments, blessings, and guiding follower nations.

| Milestone | Typical Year | Dev Required | Nations Required | Trigger Condition |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Printing Press | ~1650 | 3 | 1 | 1+ nation Dev 3 |
| Scientific Method | ~1700 | 4 | 3 | 3+ nations Dev 4 |
| Industrialization | ~1800 | 5 | 5 | 5+ nations Dev 5 |
| Electricity | ~1870 | 6 | 3 | 3+ nations Dev 6 |
| Flight | ~1910 | 7 | 1 | 1+ nation Dev 7 |
| Nuclear Power | ~1950 | 8 | 1 | 1+ nation Dev 8 |
| Computing | ~1970 | 8 | 2 | 2+ nations Dev 8 |
| Internet | ~1990 | 9 | 3 | 3+ nations Dev 9, at peace |
| Space Programs | ~2030 | 10 | 2 | 2+ nations Dev 10, cooperating |

**Development (Dev)** = nation technology level. See [World](04-world.md) for nation stats.

## Win Conditions

### Path 1: Global Cooperation

Many developed nations, shared technology, united defense. Humanity reaches space-age together. Requires commandments and playstyle that favor peace, trade, and knowledge sharing.

### Path 2: Superpower Dominance

One hyper-advanced nation carries the rest. Must reach Dev 12+ and build planetary defense alone. **Critical:** Do not destroy global infrastructure — even a superpower needs supply chains, allies, and intact rivals to absorb. Total annihilation of other nations can backfire.

### Lose Condition

Aliens arrive and humanity is not ready. Game over. New Earth.

## Mid-Era Narrative Moments

20 minutes between era transitions is a long stretch. Mid-era narrative moments provide punctuation — brief, non-interactive toasts that make the world feel alive and give the player a sense of progress. These are NOT events (no choices). They are ambient narrative.

### Milestone Toasts

| Category | Example | Trigger |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Population** | "Your followers number one million." | Player religion population crosses threshold (100K, 500K, 1M, 5M, 10M) |
| **Territory** | "Your faith is now the majority in 5 regions." | Player religion majority count crosses threshold |
| **War summary** | "The Northern War has ended. 40,000 dead. Two cities razed." | War resolves |
| **Trade** | "A new trade route connects your eastern and western followers." | Trade route forms between player-religion regions |
| **Science** | "The printing press spreads your scripture faster than any prophet." | Science milestone reached |
| **Rival** | "The followers of Solrath grow restless in the south." | Rival religion crosses a population threshold |
| **Divine** | "Your people speak of your miracle for generations." | Some time after player casts a power |
| **Development** | "Your capital has grown into a true city." | Player's origin city reaches Dev 5, 8, 10 |

### Rules

- Maximum 1 milestone toast per real-minute (don't spam)
- Toasts auto-dismiss after 4 seconds
- No player action required — these are ambient narrative, not events
- Toasts use era-appropriate language (Renaissance: "The learned men of your faith..." / Digital: "Your network of believers...")
- War summaries include casualty numbers and outcome for weight
- Each toast plays a subtle SFX chime (distinct from event notification)

## Era Transitions

When crossing era boundaries:

- **Visual shift** — palette and UI evolve (see [UI & Visuals](09-ui-and-visuals.md))
- **Event tables** — new event types unlock (see [Events](08-events.md))
- **Alien weight** — alien events only appear after ~1950; weight increases each era
- **Nation behavior** — AI adjusts to era (e.g., Enlightenment = more philosophical events, Atomic = more paranoia)

## Stage 6: Science Pacing Validation

### Timeline by Scenario (Decision 4: Always Possible With Sacrifice)

| Milestone | Normal | Optimal (peace, high dev) | Worst (constant war) |
|-----------|--------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Printing Press | ~1650 | ~1640 | ~1680 |
| Scientific Method | ~1700 | ~1680 | ~1750 |
| Industrialization | ~1800 | ~1770 | ~1860 |
| Electricity | ~1870 | ~1840 | ~1920 |
| Flight | ~1910 | ~1880 | ~1960 |
| Nuclear Power | ~1950 | ~1920 | ~2000 |
| Computing | ~1970 | ~1940 | ~2020 |
| Internet | ~1990 | ~1960 | ~2060† |
| Space Programs | ~2030 | ~2000 | ~2100† |
| Planetary Defense | ~2100 | ~2060 | ~2150† |
| Defense Grid | ~2150 | ~2120 | ~2180† |

† Both paths demand adaptation — this is the "sacrifice."

**War player levers:** Stop fighting long enough for peace milestones. Use Peace whispers (+0.15 diplomacy nudge) on warring nations. War weariness naturally pushes nations toward peace (threshold 0.50). Stop casting disaster powers for 1-2 eras to let nations develop.

**Peace player levers:** Use Science whispers on lagging nations. Cast Inspiration (+0.20 research) on low-Dev regions. Use Miracle to convert nations to your religion (shared religion = +0.2 opinion = easier cooperation). Accept that some nations need conquest by others to consolidate development.

**Verdict:** Defense Grid reachable by ~2180 even in worst case (20 years before alien arrival). Comfortable margin in normal play. Optimal play gives 80+ year buffer.
