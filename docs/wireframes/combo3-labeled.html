Complete Interaction Flow

How a player casts a blessing and toggles the overlay in one session

1
Tap FAB → Dual arcs expand. Blessings fan left (gold). Disasters fan right (dark). Eye crowns the top. Every button has a name + cost label. Map dims behind a subtle scrim.
↓
2
Tap a power (e.g., "🌾 Harvest ⚡2") → Arcs collapse. Map enters targeting mode: valid regions pulse with a gold border. Banner at bottom reads "Bountiful Harvest — tap a region to cast."
↓
3
Tap a region → Power casts. VFX plays (golden wheat waves). Energy decreases (14 → 12). Map returns to idle. Total: 3 taps.
— or, mid-cast —
2b
Tap the 👁 Divine Eye instead of a power → Arcs collapse. Overlay activates. Religion heat map, attack arrows, tension zones appear. FAB gains a purple ring. Layer picker appears at bottom.
↓
3b
Player studies the map with overlay data. Tap FAB again → Arcs reopen (overlay stays on). Now the player can see divine data while choosing which power to cast and where. Best of both worlds.

Why Labels Work

Icon-only radial menus are a known usability trap. Here's the design rationale.

Without Labels

Player sees: 🌾 💡 ✨ 🙏 🛡 👑 🌋 🌊 ☠️ ⛈ 🥀 🔥

"What does the sparkle one do? Is the skull plague or death? How much does the crown cost? Which one shields?"

Result: hesitation, wrong taps, frustration. Player closes the menu and doesn't cast.

With Labels

Player sees: 🌾 Harvest ⚡2 · 💡 Inspire ⚡3 · ✨ Miracle ⚡4 ...

"Harvest costs 2 energy, I have 14. Miracle costs 4 and converts people. I'll use that on the enemy region."

Result: confident, fast decisions. Player feels informed and divine. After 10+ opens, they stop reading labels and use spatial memory.
